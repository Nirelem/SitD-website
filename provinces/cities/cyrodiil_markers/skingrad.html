Skingrad


Skingrad during 2nd Era in Elder Scrolls Online

Skingrad is a Colovian town located in the West Weald region of Colovia, to the southwest of the Imperial City. Skingrad is known as the Gem of Old Colovia, and a model of the Colovian virtues of independence, hard work, and tough-mindedness.

Architecture & Layout: The city divides into three districts: the Castle, Hightown, and Chapel, with various gates and bridges connecting each district. The city is further divided in two parts: the north wall encompasses the Hightown district and the guild halls, while the southern half holds homes and the Great Chapel of Julianos (God of Magic), where citizens and travelers can pray for healing. Castle Skingrad sits on a very steep hill, overlooking the city. The Castle is only accessible by a long series of fortifications and bridges that connect it to the Hightown district, due to the sheer steepness of the hill making it virtually unscalable from any other side. This makes Castle Skingrad and the city as a whole very defensible against outside forces.

Politics: The town is particularly famous for its wines, tomatoes, and cheeses, which contributes to its overall prosperity. The famous Surilie Brothers wine is made from the vineyards located outside Skingrad.

Quick History: During the Great War in 4E 171, Skingrad was one of the cities to fall to the Aldmeri Dominion's invasion. When the Great War ended with the signing of the White-Gold Concordat, the Dominion relinquished all conquered territory.
