Necrom


Necrom during 2nd Era in Elder Scrolls Online

Necrom is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of Morrowind, and is perched on the Padomaic Ocean, right on the eastern edge of the Padomaic Crest. The city is most known for its massive Necropolis and complex crypts. It is called City of the Dead because it is where most Dark Elves go to commune and worship their family ancestors.

Architecture & Layout: Necrom is a dense cityscape built on a steep, mountainous island, surrounded by perilous cliffs, and at the mouth of the river that divides the southern Telvanni Peninsula region. Necrom's architectural style is noted for its lofty walls and white towers. The Necrom outskirts comprise of steep hills and winding roads strewn up and across the small river valley around it. The city's layout is like an intertwined web that meets at several points.

Politics: The city is under the rule of Great House Indoril. While most modern Dunmer have left their ancient customs behind, Necrom helps them re-connect with that lost part of their history and their ancestry.

Quick History: Morrowind in the Fourth Era has experienced a great change in its religion. After the fall of the Living-Gods, taking place after the events of Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind, the New Temple reverted to the old deities that the Dunmer used to worship before the rise of the Living-Gods. With Necrom's strong ties to the Dunmer faith, it is unknown exactly how this recent schism has affected the city.
