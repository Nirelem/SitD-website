Ald'ruhn


Aldruhn during 3rd Era in Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind

Ald'ruhn WAS one of the four cities on the district of Vvardenfell. It served as the district seat of power for the Great House Redoran. The settlement was built around the carcass of Skar, an ancient Emperor Crab that was slain by the united tribes.

Architecture & Layout: The surrounding land is inhospitable for the common-folk; it is riddled with steep hills, dead wood, and jagged rocks, and not much can be found beyond the city limits. Even so, the community of Ald'ruhn had always lived through the harsh environment, from the ash storms to the sparse grazing. Much like other House Redoran townships, the city was built in the typical Redoran village style, with organic curves and undecorated exteriors reminiscent of the environment.

There were three principal districts in Ald'ruhn. There was Ald'ruhn Town, the outer-half of the city. The second district was the Ald'ruhn-Under-Skar, which was built inside the Emperor Crab's shell. Finally, the third district was Fort Buckmoth, which housed a garrison of Imperial Legion soldiers and acted as Ald'ruhn's first line of defense.

Quick History: Unfortunately, in 4E 5, Red Mountain erupted and ravaged most of Vvardenfell. A month later, relief efforts were sent to the settlements of Vvardenfell, but it is unknown how Ald'ruhn fared during this time, or if it recovered at all. However, due to it being located near the foot of Red Mountain, it is very likely that the fate of the city has been sealed.
