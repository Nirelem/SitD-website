Balmora


Balmora during 3rd Era in Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind

Balmora was one of the four cities of the district of Vvardenfell. It was known primarily as the district seat of power for the former great house of Hlaalu, which has been replaced with the Great House Sadras.

Architecture & Layout: The city was the largest settlement on Vvardenfell, only behind the temple-metropolis, Vivec City. The city was built on the banks of the Odai River, in the midst of a great valley. Much of the land that surrounds the city is rocky scrubland, natural stone menhirs, and green hills, and provides little flatland for other people to settle on.

The city itself is split into three districts. The Commercial District is found on the western side of the river. Labor Town, on the east side of the river is the general residential district. Finally, the High Town district is on the northwestern hill that looms over the city.

Quick History: In 4E 5, Red Mountain erupted and ravaged most of Vvardenfell. A month later, relief efforts were sent to the settlements of Vvardenfell and it was subsequently rebuilt and re-inhabited.
