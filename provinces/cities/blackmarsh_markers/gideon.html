Gideon


Gideon during 2nd Era in Elder Scrolls Online

Gideon is one of the eight major cities in the province of Black Marsh, situated in the flooded hillscape of Blackwood.

Architecture & Layout: The city is surrounded by steep and lightly forested hills to the west and the humid marshland to the northeast.

The city is built majorly in the Imperial architectural style. Most of the city's buildings are located on the main road because it's the only dry ground left through Gideon, so important structures such as the Temple of Dibella (the Goddess of Beauty) and the Governor's Mansion can be found here. While the main road is large and spacious, the streets of Gideon are described as cramped and labyrinthine.

Politics: Gideon is a sleepy frontier town that serves as a gateway to the greater and treacherous expanse of the province. It is a mixture of two cultures, from the modern-day Imperials to the Argonians of Blackwood. Gideon is often visited and settled by those that seek out Black Marsh's wonders.

Quick History: Early in the 4th Era, Gideon was overrun by the undead forces of Umbriel, a floating island-city that required the souls of the dead to stay afloat. Fortunately, Gideon has mostly recovered as of the current day.
