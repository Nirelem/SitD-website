Lilmoth


Lilmoth before Imperial control, 2nd Era in Elder Scrolls Online

Lilmoth is one of the eight major cities in the province of Black Marsh, serving as the most centralized settlement in the region of Murkmire. Lilmoth is colloquially known as the "Festering Jewel of Black Marsh". The city is known for the sunken Imperial villas that were built during the Second Empire's hold over the region.

Architecture & Layout: Lilmoth is built on wetlands that make up the southern region of Murkmire, and is surrounded by rainforests. Being built over shallow waters, large ships are unable to dock at the city and are instead required to anchor in the Southern Sea, remaining close enough to move goods.

Lilmoth is a walled-off settlement, surrounded by traditional Imperial walls for the most part. Despite the outward appearance of an Imperial settlement, most of Lilmoth's architecture is Argonian, using a style of building made for the marshes of Murkmire. These structures are supported by wooden stilts, which sometimes extend high into the air over the city. In comparison, Imperial homes built on that soil quickly sank into the swamp. Nearer the walls are a few intact Imperial buildings. Old Imperial Lilmoth is a district filled with ancient Imperial ruins, old encrusted villas festooned with vines, large bamboos, and sleeping palms.

Politics: After the events of Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion, an Argonian nationalist, anti-foreigner political party called the An-Xileel rose to power. The An-Xileel in Lilmoth wrestled control of the city from the Third Empire. Foreigner inhabitants were permitted to stay but were treated with scrutiny by An-Xileel Argonians. Imperial institutions were removed from Lilmoth, and the Old Imperial Lilmoth district was looted.

Quick History: Early in the 4th Era, Lilmoth was ravaged by the undead forces of Umbriel, a floating island-city that required the souls of the dead to stay afloat. The An-Xileel were able to take back the city but all of the foreigners and non-An-Xileel Argonians were killed.
