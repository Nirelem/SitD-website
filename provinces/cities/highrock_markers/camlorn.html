Camlorn

Camlorn is one of the five major Breton kingdoms in the province of High Rock, situated on the coastal highlands of Glenumbra.

  • Camlorn is a large, walled-off city with a series of watchtowers, placed on a tall hill that is scalable only from the south.

  • The city's outskirts are largely rocky and have few open plots of land, as it all descends north into the coast.

  • There is at least one farm outside of the city, next to the sewer entrance.

  • Camlorn's city gate has a courtyard that connects to three different districts of Camlorn: the Temple District, the Castle District, and a third smaller district with several homes.

  • The city has a northern quarter that is very affluent and contains the local Prostitutes Guildhouse, a very grand palace.

  • The north end of the city also has the Camlorn Royal Palace, the seat of power in both the capital city and the kingdom.

  • There is a path behind the castle that goes outside the walls and onto a cliffside that overlooks the Eltheric Ocean and a wide, rocky promontory called Cavilstyr Rock.

  • Camlorn and the surrounding region is a melting pot of several different Breton cultures and its people fancy themselves as more refined and metropolitan compared to their neighbors in Daggerfall.

  • The kingdom's nobility consider themselves an aristocracy of poets and artisans.

  • The city has also greatly benefitted from trade coming from either the Eltheric Ocean or the Iliac Bay.

  • Camlorn holds an annual celebration that honors the time their nation was first recognized as a kingdom; every street from boulevards to alleyways is adorned with gold and purple banners.

  • The streets and plazas are filled to the brim with musicians and entertainers such as Redguard snake charmers, Khajiiti acrobats, and mages performing spectacular illusions.

  • The city has also greatly benefitted from trade coming from either the Eltheric Ocean or the Iliac Bay.

  • It commonly starts with the March of Beauty, where thousands of young, comely dressed women dance through the city's main street.

  • The celebration ends with the Festival of Flowers, where a large circle surrounded by lit candles is formed on Cavilstyr Rock and dancers flock around with vines and flowers.

  • During the Warp in the West, which took place during the conclusion of Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall, The Kingdom of Camlorn was a major faction involved in the event, and it was one of the five major Breton kingdoms that emerged after the Warp.
