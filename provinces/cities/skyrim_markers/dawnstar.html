Dawnstar


Dawnstar during 4th Era in Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

Dawnstar (also known as Danstrar) is a settlement on the northern coast of Skyrim, and the hold capital of the Pale. It was once a garrison town, which served as an exile post due to the cold harsh climate and notorious gales. The settlement seemingly got its name due to its reputation for "greeting the sun as it begins its journey".

Architecture & Layout:The city has two mines, Iron-Breaker Mine and Quicksilver Mine, which supply iron and quicksilver respectively. It is home to many fishermen and is an important port. An ancient Dark Brotherhood sanctuary, Tamriel's most infamous assassins guild, is located beneath the city. The White Hall, a large longhouse located in Dawnstar, is the seat of the Jarl of the Pale. The Tower of Dawn, also called Nightcaller Temple, is a ruined military fort often used as a place of worship and sits upon a cliff top overlooking the town. A small lighthouse sits atop a hill just east of town.

Politics: Because of the glacial icefields just to the east, it is the last port before Windhelm that is not icebound following the Great Collapse. Its economy is driven by its two mines.

Quick History: During the Skyrim Civil War, which took place in Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim, the city of Dawnstar was aligned with the Stormcloak Rebellion. The once abandoned Dark Brotherhood sanctuary would become inhabited once again. It would serve as the last refuge for the crippled guild of assassins, as all their other sanctuaries have been destroyed.
