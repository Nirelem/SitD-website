Solitude


Upper Solitude, 4th Era in Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

Solitude is one of the nine major cities in the province of Skyrim, situated on the highland bluffs of Haafingar Hold.

Layout & Architecture: Solitude is one of Skyrim's largest cities, as well as one of its key ports, built on a natural landmark known as the Great Arch. The busier sections of Solitude, such as the crowded harbors on the coast, are built underneath the Great Arch, next to the Karth River. Because of the uneven terrain outside of the city walls, there is very little room for private land for farms.

The road leading up to Solitude's entrance is heavily defended by layers of gates and fortifications. Solitude contains a unique style of architecture only seen in Haafingar, consisting of rounded architecture and pointed roofs. The upper city's western district is known as the Well District. To the north, on the mountainside is Castle Dour, the city fortress that contains the Temple of the Eight Divines and the Emperor's Tower. The city's eastern district, known as the Avenues District is the residential area, known for its regal and grandiose streets. The Bards College, the Hall of the Dead, and the seat of power, the Blue Palace are located here.

Both the east and west sides are separated by an arching bridge that connects the Castle Dour to the Windmill, one of Solitude's most iconic landmarks. Its wind power is used to open the massive warehouse that is stored underneath the mountain. There is a vast cavern underneath the mountain that is currently used by the East Empire Company as one of their largest warehouses.

Politics: Solitude is currently the capital of Skyrim. The city is a major trading capital in the Sea of Ghosts, with an expansive and illustrious history that gives it its prominent political power. It is the home of the Bards College, an institute that preserves the ancient Nordic traditions of music and story-telling. Solitude is the Imperial seat of power in Skyrim and has been the most influenced by Imperial culture since the union of King Mantiarco and Queen Potema Septim.

Quick History: During the events of Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim, the former High King of Skyrim was the late ruler of Solitude, Torygg. When the Jarl of Windhelm and leader of the Stormcloak Rebellion, Ulfric Stormcloak, killed High King Torygg, it exploded into the Skyrim Civil War. Solitude was the headquarters of the pro-Empire faction, with General Tullius commanding the Imperial Legion from Castle Dour, while the newly appointed Jarl Elisif would continue governing the hold of Haafingar from the Blue Palace.
