Hegathe


Hegathe during 3rd Era in Elder Scrolls I: Arena

Hegathe is one of the eight major cities in the province of Hammerfell. It was once the capital of Hammerfell, ever since the Yokudans landed on its shores back in the First Era.

Architecture & Layout: The city is found in the fertile grasslands on the tropical western shore landings of the Abecean Sea. The entire peninsula that the city itself is found upon is a lush subtropical rainforest biome. It is known for its austere ramparts that harken back to antiquity, and is considered an equal to Wayrest in terms of splendor. Like other traditional cities in Hammerfell, it is built atop of old ruins of past civilizations, replaced with high domes and flying dew sails that are well-suited for the harsh environment. It is a city of contrasts with tall, plain stone ramparts found side-by-side with beautiful gold-engraved tents, and a busy modern harbor found near the calm ancient shrines. Thanks to its coastal plains environment, the city has vineyards where they make their signature wine.

Politics: One of the city's most iconic landmarks is the Shrine of Tava(a Redguard deity, Goddess of the weather), a place of veneration for both the Crowns and the Forebears. The site symbolizes an ideal: the union of the two factions. Being on the southern part of Hammerfell, it is probably under the influence of the Forebears.

Quick History: During the Great War, Hegathe was being defended by a Crown army. When it was besieged by Aldmeri Dominion forces, a relief army of Forebears arrived and lifted the siege. This victory brought the reconciliation of the two parties and was start of the tenuous peace that they have today.
