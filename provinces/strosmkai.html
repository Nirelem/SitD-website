Stros M'kai


Stros M'Kai is a strategically located tropical island off the southern coast of Hammerfell near the Cape of the Blue Divide. Palm trees, large leafed plants and cacti are a common sight on Stros M'Kai. Jarrin root, an extremely toxic plant which kills almost instantly when ingested, is native to the island.

It is renowned for the completely steam-powered Dwemer ruins of Bthzark and the observatory of similar design containing a telescope and orrery. Roughly a third of the island is taken up by the Ogres Tooth Mountains in the west, containing the ruins of Bthzark. Its mines and other facilities delve into the southern half of the range, while the steam and gas harvesting system that powers the mechanisms inside lie above ground in the northern mountains.

Its main two settlements are the city of Port Hunding, which has also been called Stros M'Kai at times, and the small settlement of Saintsport that is found at the southern tip of the island.

Quick History: During the Great War, the Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai was a treaty signed in 4E 180 between the Aldmeri Dominion and the Redguards of Hammerfell. It marked the end of a decade-long attempt by the Dominion to conquer the region after both sides had reached a stalemate. The conditions of the treaty compelled the Dominion to withdraw all of its military forces from Hammerfell. Following the Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai, Hammerfell was left an independent yet severely diminished nation.

Port Hunding

Port Hunding from a mod for Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind

Port Hunding, also called Stros M'Kai, is a city on the island of Stros M'Kai. Situated north of Hunding Bay, the city takes up a large portion of the island's area.

Architecture & Layout: Port Hunding is divided into roughly four districts: the harbor, market, town square, and old quarter. Ships entering the harbor are greeted by a large metal statue of Frandar Hunding, considered the most accomplished Sword-singer and one of the first Yokudans to settle in Hammerfell, with his sword extending out to sea. The old quarter lies northeast of the town square. A large palace is built north of the square on a section of land separated by a river.
