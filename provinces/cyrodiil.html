Cyrodiil

Summary:

  • Homeland of the Imperials.

  • Makes up the center of Tamriel.

  • The center of the empire and seat of governance, Cyrodiil is also known as "the Heartland".

  • At its heart stands the White-Gold Tower, possibly the most renowned monument in Tamriel.

  • Borders Skyrim to the north, Hammerfell to the north west, Morrowind to the northeast, Black Marsh to the southeast, Elsweyr to the south, and Valenwood to the southwest.

Geographic features:

  • Most of Cyrodiil is similar to Southern Europe, with temperate climate and vast fertile farmlands and vineyards.

  • It is a diverse region, featuring rolling plains, dense forests, marshy swamps in the southeast, and the towering Jerall Mountains in the north.

  • The land is paved with a far-reaching and well-maintained road network, branching out from the Imperial city and stretching to the other cities and the provinces beyond.

  • Cyrodiil can be geographically divided into two parts:

    • Colovia, which makes up the western half. (west of the Niben River)
    • Nibenay, which makes up the eastern half. (east of the Niben River)
    • These areas make up either side of the great Niben River, the large river that cuts through the middle of Cyrodiil.
    • Cyrodiil has a varied environment, and is divided into several regions:
    • Nibenay Basin:
      • This vast region dominates Cyrodiil, containing all the area that drains into the River Niben.
      • East of the river, the region consists of mostly open fields while to the west and north it is more wooded.
      • Contains the city of Cheydinhal.
