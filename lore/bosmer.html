Wood Elves / Bosmer


Illustration of a Bosmer male

Valenwood

The Bosmer, or Wood Elves, are the Elven clan-folk of Valenwood.

Summary:

  • Small in stature, compared to the other races.

  • Lifespan two to three times as long as humans.

  • Best archers in all Tamriel, they snatch and release arrows in one continuous motion.

  • Very agile, making them excellent scouts and thieves.

  • Affinity for animals, with some Bosmer able to command simple-minded creatures.

  • Have a nearly chameleon-like ability to hide in forested areas.

  • Many, but not all, adhere to the Green Pact, which forbids consuming or destroying any form of vegetation.


Bosmer society is deeply tied to the natural world, with a lifestyle centered around harmony with the forest and a deep respect for the Green Pact, a sacred agreement with the forest god Y’ffre. This pact forbids the Bosmer from harming or consuming plant life in Valenwood, leading them to rely on hunting, trading, and creative use of bone, animal products, and imported goods for sustenance and craftsmanship. Despite their communal ties to nature, their society is also fragmented into clans, with no strict central authority, making their alliances and loyalties quite fluid.

Religion

The Bosmer venerate many other deities, from Aedra (gods) to Daedra (demons) to other spirits unique to Valenwood. Most Bosmer claim direct descent from Auri-El, though Y'ffre is their most important deity. like the Argonians, the Bosmer seem to prefer to live in the present, showing relatively little regard for the ways of the past (besides when it comes to telling old tales or following the Green Pact, that is).

  • Y’ffre (Jephre): The central deity and creator of the Bosmer. Known as the Forest God, Y’ffre established the Green Pact, binding the Bosmer to protect and live in harmony with the forests of Valenwood. Y’ffre also shaped the world after its chaotic beginnings, making him a figure of order and stability.

  • Auri-El: The Elven King and god of time, equivalent to Akatosh in other cultures' religion. To the Bosmer, Auri-El represents the connection to their shared Aldmeri heritage and is revered as a distant, guiding force.

  • Arkay: The god of life and death, honored as the guardian of the natural cycle. The Bosmer invoke Arkay in burial rites and see him as the protector of souls after death.

  • Xarxes: The god of secret knowledge and is worshipped by Bosmeri mages who wish to learn unnatural arcane arts.

  • Mara: The goddess of love and fertility, just as in many other cultures. To the Bosmer, she is the wife of Auri-El.

  • Stendarr: The god of compassion and rightouesness, though to Bosmer he is known also as the "Apologist of Man".

  • Baan Dar: A trickster deity, introduced to Valenwood from cultural exchange with the Khajiit. He's linked with good-will, cleverness, and is a god of archery which leads to many Bosmer seeking out his guidance.

  • Z'en: The god of vengeance and payment in kind, and is often invoked by Treethanes in the name of justice, or to end unnecessary vendetas.

The Silvenar and The Green Lady

The Silvenar and the Green Lady are sacred figures in Bosmer culture, embodying the spiritual and physical aspects of the Bosmer people and their connection to Valenwood.

While the Silvenar is often viewed by outsiders as a title for the Bosmer's political representative for foreign affairs, he is more than a mere politician. The Silvenar is a spiritual leader and symbolic representative of the Bosmer’s collective voice, often serving as a mediator and diplomat. The Silvenar actually feels the will of the people and acts upon it, and the people likewise can feel the Silvenar's influence and be swayed by it. The Silvenar has many rights which grant him great authority in Valenwood. For one, only the Silvenar can grant foreign building and trade contracts, meaning any foreign power which wishes to build in Valenwood or do business with the Bosmer must deal with the Silvenar first.

The Green Lady, on the other hand, represents the raw physicality and primal energy of the Bosmer, embodying their strength, passion, and ferocity in defending the forest and their traditions. If the Silvenar dies, his Green Lady's bestial nature is irrevocably unleashed, and her fury turned on all those involved until she joins him in death.

Together, the two are bonded through a mystical union that represents Valenwood as a whole. Upon the death of either the Silvenar or the Green Lady, a successor is chosen, ensuring their roles persist and the balance between spirit and nature is maintained.

The Wild Hunt

(This is more on the obscure side of lore and will most likely not be relevant to our game ...probably.)

The Wild Hunt is a magical trait innate to all Bosmer. It is brought on by a collective ritual performed by many Bosmer, causing them to shapeshift into a horde of feral, eldritch beasts. This transformation cannot be undone. These monsters stampede, changing their form constantly, slaying and devouring all in their path, devouring the flesh of those in their way to the bone in seconds, and eventually, when left with no surviving targets, turn upon themselves in a "cannibalistic orgy". This transformation is seen by the Bosmer as a return to the chaos of the Dawn Era they call the Ooze, a state of existence where all life and land on Nirn were formless and shifted constantly between shapes, unable to hold one form for more than a moment.
