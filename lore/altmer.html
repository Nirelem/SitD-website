High Elves / Altmer


Illustration of an Altmer male

Summerset Isles

The Altmer, or High Elves, are a tall, golden-skinned race, hailing from Summerset Isle.

Summary:

  • Generally viewed as proud or snobbish.

  • Lifespan two to three times as long as humans.

  • Affinity for the arcane arts.

  • Resisatance to disease damage.

  • But they are also vulnerable to magic.


Every elven race can trace back their lineage to the ancient Aldmer of the lost continent of Aldmeris, but it is believed that the Altmer are the most direct, unaltered descendants of the Aldmer. Because of this, purity and preserving one's bloodline is important in the upper circles of Altmer society. This emphasis on perfection, honor, and nobility reflects on every aspect of their society, from their art and architecture to their arms and armor. However, this obsession also has its darker sides. Over hundreds of generations, the High Elves attempt to breed themselves into a racially pure line. They do not reproduce as often or as quickly as humans do. Incest is not uncommon, and it is said that 9 out of 10 babies are discarded after being born, simply due to some form of "defect". Altmeri names are simply combinations of numbers in their own language, and Altmer culture revolves around displaying social status.

Many Altmer of the Summerset Isles prefer to live an isolationist life on their idyllic shores, viewing themselves as superior and more advanced, technologically and magically, compared to the other races. Exile to the mainland is seen as equivalent to a death sentence. If you are an Altmer from outside Summerset, you were either exiled there, or were born in a family that, at one point, was originally from the Summerset Isles.

Religion

The Altmer pantheon consists primarily of Aedra (which are like the 'gods' in the Elder Scrolls and are the opposite of the Daedra). The Altmer believe that they are descended from the Aedra, and view them as their divine ancestors. The Altmer focus on their divine lineage and strive to emulate the purity and greatness of their ancestors, believing themselves to be the closest to the gods among the mortal races.

  • Auri-El: The chief deity, equivalent to Akatosh in other cultures' religion. Auri-El is the god of time, the king of the Aedra, and a symbol of Elven purity and their connection to divinity.

  • Jephre/Y'ffre: The god of nature, revered for his role in shaping the natural world and teaching the Altmer to live in harmony with it.

  • Magnus: Magnus led the Aedra in creating the Mundus but fled before its completion, tearing a hole in reality that became the sun.

  • Mara: The goddess of love, fertility, and compassion, promoting unity and family among the Altmer.

  • Phynaster: The god of longevity and exploration, teaching the Altmer ways to extend their lives and traverse the world safely.

  • Stendarr: The god of mercy and justice, associated with compassion and fair rule.

  • Syrabane: The god of magic and wisdom, who is often credited with teaching the Altmer many magical arts and aiding in their survival during early eras.

  • Trinimac: A warrior god of strength and honor, revered as a cultural hero. He was later corrupted into becoming Malacath, the god and creator of the Orcs.

  • Xarxes: The god of history and memory, who records the deeds of the Altmer and helps preserve their legacy.

  • Zenithar: The god of labor, commerce, and prosperity, although less prominent in Altmer culture compared to human worship.

  • Lorkhan (as a cautionary figure): While not worshipped, Lorkhan is remembered as the trickster god who deceived the Aedra into creating the mortal plane, which the Altmer see as a flawed, imprisoning reality.

The Thalmor and the Aldmeri Dominion

The Thalmor are the ruling political organization of the Aldmeri Dominion, which both came about in the early Fourth Era. They are an elitist and authoritarian group of Altmer who believe in the superiority of the Elven races and seek to restore their ancient dominance over Tamriel.

This political party has existed for most of the Altmer's history, but they were usually just a very small party and only represented a minority of society. However, much like how the Nazi party rose to power in our world, the Thalmor became more and more popular after Summerset was beset by crises and turmoil, following the Oblivion Crisis from Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion. At the turn of the Fourth Era was the rise of the Thalmor, who instituted a policy of persecution and even open slaughter of dissidents and non-Elven races in Summerset. Numerous surviving Altmer dissidents were forced to flee to other parts of Tamriel, where they were frequently targeted by Thalmor assassins. While many Altmer continue to disagree with the Thalmor in private, few venture to oppose them publicly.

The Thalmor are infamous for their efforts to suppress Talos worship (God-Hero of Man and founder of the Empire), which they view as heretical due to their disdain for the divinization of a human. They also hunt down anyone who expresses any form of dislike towards the Thalmor or the Aldmeri Dominion. Due to the reputationn of their strict rule and ruthless tactics, the Thalmor are a major antagonist force in Tamriel's recent history.

Their goals extend beyond political control, aiming to undo the creation of Mundus, which they see as a flawed, mortal prison.
