Imperials


Illustration of an Imperial male

Cyrodiil

Also known as Cyrodils, the well-educated and well-spoken Imperials are the natives of the civilized, cosmopolitan province of Cyrodiil.

Summary:

  • Known to be shrewd diplomats and traders, and for their respect for the rule of law.

  • Average human lifespan (70-80 years).

  • The discipline and training of their citizen armies are widely respected and feared throughout Tamriel.

  • These traits, along with their remarkable Legions, allowed them to untie Tamriel under one banner and found the Empire.

  • They are both strong warriors and charismatic leaders, able to command the attention of a group of people.

  • Romans


Cyrodiil was once ruled by a race of elves known as the Ayleids. These elves enslaved the native humans, called Nedes, and they were cruel slavemasters. These Nedes would end up revolting and overthrowing their elven tyrants. This rebellion was led by Alessia, who is now known as Saint Alessia the Slave Queen, along with the help from the Nords of Skyrim.

The defining feature of Imperial culture is its cosmopolitanism. It takes elements from Colovia, Nibenay, High Rock, and even a few elements from the far away continent of Akavir, and incorporates them into a "common whole". Although some individual Imperials may be the most arrogant bigots in Tamriel, culturally the Imperials are very conciliatory and congenial, making them skilled diplomats and traders.

The Imperials, and Cyrodiil as a whole, are divided into two major culture groups: the Colovians in western Colovia and the Nibenese in eastern Nibenay.

The Colovians are known for being practical, militaristic, and independent-minded. They prioritize loyalty, discipline, and physical strength, producing many of the Empire's soldiers and generals. Their culture is more austere, with less emphasis on religion and mysticism compared to the Nibenese.

The Nibenese culture is heavily influenced by ancient Ayleid practices, mysticism, and a pantheon of gods, blending Imperial and Elven elements. They emphasize trade, art, and scholarship, often relying on diplomacy and wealth rather than brute strength. They tend to produce the Empire's battlemages, governors, and merchants.

Empires

Imperial hegemony has waxed and waned throughout the eras, and most historians refer to three distinct Empires, the ends of which each mark a new epoch in Tamrielic history.

  • First Empire: Also called the Alessian Empire, the First Empire was established by the former human slaves of the Ayleid Empire in 1E 243 following Alessia's Slave Rebellion. One of the defining events of the Empire was the rise of the zealously monotheistic Alessian Order. They were a sect of human supremacists and were very against all races of elves. The First Empire ended in 1E 2331.

  • Second Empire: The Second Empire was established in 1E 2703 by Reman Cyrodiil I. At its height, it controlled every region of Tamriel with the exception of Morrowind. The history of the Second Empire is divided into two main periods: the reign of the Reman Dynasty, and the subsequent Akaviri Potentate. The Reman Dynasty was founded after he defeated an Akaviri invasion of Tamriel in 1E 2703. The surviving Akaviri would swear fealty to Reman, and ended up forming the elite bodyguard unit of the Emperor, the Blades. The Reman Dynasty's end at 1E 2920 would mark the last year of the First Era and the start of the Second Era, but the Second Empire would still endure after this. The Akaviri Potentate would continue to rule the Second Empire at the start of the Second Era until 2E 430, which was the end of the Second Empire.

  • Third Empire: The Third Empire was declared by Tiber Septim in 2E 854, the last year of the Second Era, after a decades-long conflict to unite Tamriel knowns as the Tiber War. It achieved what the Second Empire could not, and united every region in Tamriel under one banner. After Tiber Septim's death, he became revered as a god, and was added to the pantheon of the Eight Divines, which soon became the Nine Divines. His deified image was renamed to Talos, the God-Hero of Mankind. The Septim Dynasty ended in 3E 433 when Martin Septim sacrificed himself at the end of Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion. The end of the Septim bloodline marked the end of the Third Era, but the Empire still endured into the Fourth Era.

Religion

Imperials primarily worship the Divines, which consist of the original Eight Divines plus the ascended Talos. Together, they make the Nine Divines. However, in recent years, Talos worship was banned due to the peace deal that ended the Great War.

Nine Divines

  • Akatosh: Akatosh, known as Auri-El to the elves, is the Dragon God of Time and chief deity of the Divines. He represents endurance, stability, and the passage of time and is revered as the father of dragons and a symbol of the Empire’s legitimacy.

  • Arkay: The God of Life and Death, overseeing the natural cycle of birth, life, and the afterlife. He is honored in burial rites and as the protector of the sanctity of life.

  • Dibella: The Goddess of Beauty, Love, and Art. She inspires creativity, passion, and the appreciation of life’s pleasures, often associated with romantic and physical love.

  • Julianos: The God of Wisdom and Logic, revered as a patron of learning, knowledge, and law. He is particularly venerated by scholars, mages, and those who value reason and study.

  • Kynareth: Known as Kyne to the Nords, she is the Goddess of the Winds and Nature. She is the patron of travelers and the natural world, often associated with the sky and the forces of weather.

  • Mara: The Goddess of Love and Compassion, symbolizing familial bonds, marriage, and motherhood. She is widely revered as a nurturing and protective figure.

  • Stendarr: The God of Mercy, Justice, and Righteous Might. He is venerated as a protector of the weak and an upholder of fair judgment and compassion.

  • Zenithar: The God of Work, Commerce, and Prosperity. He represents honest labor and trade, encouraging fairness and success through diligence and integrity.

  • Talos: A mortal and emperor who upon death became a divine, the respect and admiration Imperials had for Tiber Septim is nothing compared to the veneration they have for him as a god of the Nine Divines, regardless of the heresies often espoused about him.

The Great War

The Great War was fought from 4E 171 to 175. It's conclusion was a terrible blow for the Empire, and it's effects are still felt in the present day. The most notable of these are:

  • The banning of Talos worship.

  • The disbandment of the Blades.

  • Legal immunity for Thalmor Justiciars to patrol Imperial territory and enforce the terms of the treaty.


The Great War is perhaps one of the most important recent historical events that are relevant to our campaign.
