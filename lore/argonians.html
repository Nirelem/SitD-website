Argonians


Illustration of an Argonian male

Black Marsh

The Argonians are the reptilian native race of Black Marsh.

Summary:

  • Argonians are created by and have an almost symbiotic connection to a species of giant sapient trees, known as the Hist.

  • Viewed as 'alien' to most outsiders; often called strange, expressionless, and hard to know.

  • Agile and cunning, they are known for excelling in stealth and guerilla tactics.

  • Can breathe underwater and are fast swimmers.

  • Natural immunity to most diseases and poisons.


Argonian society is deeply rooted in the swamps of Black Marsh, characterized by its tribal organization and unique connection to the Hist. Tribes are the central social structure, with leaders and shamans often acting as intermediaries between the Argonians and the Hist. Their culture values adapting to change and a deep spiritual connection to nature. The warm and fetid swamps of Black Marsh are toxic to most outsiders, except for the Argonians who have evolved to be adapted to it. Argonians hatch from eggs, and it is the Hist that forms their biology, molding them into creatures that can withstand the environment of Black Marsh.

Argonians tend to speak with a certain detachment that might seem particularly strange to individuals with a formal upbringing centered around manners. Argonians do not facially express their emotions as much as men and mer do, though anger is fairly easy to detect through bared teeth and narrowed eyes.

There are two types of Argonians: those native to Black Marsh, and those either born and/or raised outside of Black Marsh. The latter is refered to as lukiul, which means assimilated to another culture. Lukiul Argonians have an unclear connection to the Hist, due to the absence of Hist sap, which is an essential part to communing with the Hist. Argonians who leave Black Marsh and spend a prolonged period of time away from the Hist may feel their connection to the Hist weaken, and may even be unable to hear the Hist at all. Lukiul have difficulty understanding the body language of marsh-born Argonians, and they are often treated as outsiders by the natives of Black Marsh.

For millenia, the Dunmer have raided the lands of Black Marsh for slave labor. Entire tribes were dragged off in chains to Morrowind. The Dunmeri Great House Dres had been cemented as a Great House for their efficiency in conducting slave-raids, and becoming the primary providers for the slave trade in their homeland. While some Dark Elves saw the enslavement of the Argonians as a great uplifting and betterment for the race in general, the Lizard Folk themselves detested their so-called masters and resisted them constantly.

However, in the late Third Era, the government of Morrowind would eventually abolish the enslavement of other races and deemed it as illegal. With the abolition of slavery, the Argonians' relationship with the Dunmer vastly improved, although violent attempts to reclaim the lands stolen from them would still continue.

Religion

The Hist

The Hist is the central focus of Argonian spirituality and religion. The Hist are sentient, ancient trees that are believed to be the progenitors of the Argonians, providing guidance to their people. The Hist's roots run deep beneath the soil of Black Marsh, connecting them all, and thus connecting all Argonians. As the Hist are all connected at the roots, they have a collective consciousness, and can communicate with each other. Argonians see themselves as extensions of the Hist’s will, often communing with the trees through rituals and consuming Hist sap during key life events, such as adulthood or bonding ceremonies. The Hist are also said to influence Argonian physiology, determining their traits and abilities based on the needs of the Hist and the environment. Argonian religion is not dogmatic or hierarchical but deeply personal and tied to their everyday lives, making it distinct from the organized religions of other Tamrielic races.

During the Oblivion Crisis in 3E 433, the Hist trees foresaw this coming disaster and called many Argonians back to defend their homeland. The Argonians were altered by the Hist in order to combat the Daedra (demons), becoming faster, stronger, and able to endure harsher punishment. When the Daedra launched their invasion of Black Marsh, the Lizard Folk fought them back so viciously that the Daedra themselves were forced to close their portals to Black Marsh, to avoid being overrun. Because of this, the Argonians emerged from the Oblivion Crisis more united and stronger than ever before compared to the other Tamrielic races, who were devastated by the crisis.

The An-Xileel and the Accession War

The An-Xileel is a political and military faction that rose to prominence during and after the Oblivion Crisis, becoming the de facto ruling body of Black Marsh. They are fiercely nationalistic and dedicated to protecting Argonian sovereignty, often viewing foreign powers like the Empire and the Dunmer as threats. The An-Xileel are closely tied to the Hist, with their policies and actions heavily influenced by the trees’ guidance. Their leadership during the Oblivion Crisis was critical, as they coordinated the Argonians’ aggressive defense of Black Marsh. Under the An-Xileel, Black Marsh has become increasingly isolationist, emphasizing independence and the preservation of Argonian culture over integration with the rest of Tamriel.

In 4E 5, Red Mountain in Morrowind erupted and threw the province into disarray. The An-Xileel took advantage of this opportunity and, in the following year of 4E 6, they launched an invasion into Morrowind which would be called the Accession War. Their invasion devastated much of southern and eastern Morrowind, and they even sacked the former capital city of Mournhold. The war would end when the Argonians were driven out by the armies of House Redoran.
