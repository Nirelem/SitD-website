Bretons


Illustration of a Breton male

High Rock

The Bretons are a hybrid race of both human and elven ancestry that primarily inhabit their ancestral homeland of High Rock.

Summary:

  • Compared to other human races, Bretons have more rounded and softer features, being less muscular than Nords and Redguards.

  • Average human lifespan (70-80 years).

  • They have a mix of the magical affinity of their elven ancestors, but also the endurance and hardiness of humans.

  • Known for being intelligent, individualistic, energetic, pragmatic, and humorous people.

  • Also known for their numerous knightly orders, and their romanticized sense of honor and freedom.

  • For the sake of brevity, you can just think of them as the typical feudalistic medieval Europe fantasy trope. (their lore goes way deeper, but this is just a summary)


High Rock is fractious and divided politically, made of up of five major kingdoms in the present day. It used to be much more fragmented with dozens of minor kingdoms, and warfare between these kingdoms accounts for much of Breton history. Despite this, Bretons across the province share many facets of culture, including language, bardic traditions, and heroic tales. Bretons make up the peasantry, soldiery, magical elite, and ruling families of the feudal kingdoms competing for power within High Rock, but the race can also be found around the rest of Tamriel, often as merchants or mercenaries.

The Breton race originated from the aboriginal human race, known as Nedes, interbreeding with Altmer colonists that settled in the lands of modern High Rock. This particular group of Altmer that would become ingrained in High Rock are known as Clan Direnni. The Direnni left a significant mark on the Bretons. The race's obsession with nobility, status, and lineage can be traced back to their days as vassals of Clan Direnni. After millennia of interbreeding, the humanistic features of the Bretons slowly overpowered their elven features. In the present day, Clan Direnni is but a small and minor group found only in the tiny Isle of Balfiera, while the Bretons have replaced them as the rulers of what is now High Rock.

Bretons possess an inborn and instinctive bond for the magical arts, often attributed to their elven blood. They are known for their intelligence and have a quick and perceptive grasp of spellcraft, enchantment, and alchemy. Magic permeates throughout all of Breton society, from the serf all the way up to the king; the average Breton mage is said to perform "flamboyant gesticulations" when spellcasting.

Religion

Bretons primarily worship the Imperial pantheon of the Eight Divines, but some still worship certain elven deities, which are an influence from the time of Clan Direnni.

Eight Divines

  • Akatosh: Akatosh, known as Auri-El to the elves, is the Dragon God of Time and chief deity of the Divines. He represents endurance, stability, and the passage of time and is revered as the father of dragons and a symbol of the Empire’s legitimacy.

  • Arkay: The God of Life and Death, overseeing the natural cycle of birth, life, and the afterlife. He is honored in burial rites and as the protector of the sanctity of life.

  • Dibella: The Goddess of Beauty, Love, and Art. She inspires creativity, passion, and the appreciation of life’s pleasures, often associated with romantic and physical love.

  • Julianos: The God of Wisdom and Logic, revered as a patron of learning, knowledge, and law. He is particularly venerated by scholars, mages, and those who value reason and study.

  • Kynareth: Known as Kyne to the Nords, she is the Goddess of the Winds and Nature. She is the patron of travelers and the natural world, often associated with the sky and the forces of weather.

  • Mara: The Goddess of Love and Compassion, symbolizing familial bonds, marriage, and motherhood. She is widely revered as a nurturing and protective figure.

  • Stendarr: The God of Mercy, Justice, and Righteous Might. He is venerated as a protector of the weak and an upholder of fair judgment and compassion.

  • Zenithar: The God of Work, Commerce, and Prosperity. He represents honest labor and trade, encouraging fairness and success through diligence and integrity.

Other Deities

  • Jephre/Y'ffre: God of the Forest and the spirit of the "now". Jephre is typically considered an elven god, but is likely inherited from the time of the Direnni. While he is revered by some Breton hunters and farmers, his worship is most prevalent among the Breton druids. (see below)

  • Phynaster: Phynaster is an Altmer hero-god who is the patron deity and teacher of the Direnni. He is often worshipped by Breton mages who care about their elven blood.

  • Sheor: Also known as the Bad Man, Sheor is the source of all strife. He is believed to have been introduced by the elves during their conflicts with the Nords as a demonized version of Shor.

Knightly Orders

Breton society is famous for its knightly orders, with notable examples including Daggerfall's Knights of the Dragon, the Knights of the Rose of Wayrest, the Knights of the Flame of Anticlere, and Dwynnen's Order of the Raven. High Rock's cultural history is founded on tales of such noble and chivalrous knights. The knightly orders were founded to carry on the tradition of nobles-in-arms and to ensure the province would have defenders at the ready should invading armies come to their gates. Knights are addressed with the honor of "sir" or "dame." In most cases, these knightly orders are governed by a marshal or a knight-commander.

Druids

When the Breton race was still new and emerging, druids were the first to lead them. Druidic spellcraft became the first truly Breton creation, and "druid" as a distinct identity was fully realized. These druids believe in the Breton's ability to enhance or perfect nature.

Somewhere along the line, a split occurred among these druids, and eventually the witch covens of the Wyrd diverged from the True Way in a religious schism. While the druids revere nature, they were not keen to isolate themselves and worship the untamed wilds. Therein lies the primary difference in culture between druids and the Wyrd: the Wyrd abandoned civilization in favor of living in the wilds, and believe that they are nature itself. In contrast, druids accepted what they interpreted as their "proper place" as people, and embraced the idea that civilization can coexist alongside nature. The druids found the ways of the Wyrd unsavory, and the Wyrd thought the druids were no better than city-dwellers.
