Redguards


Illustration of a Redguard male

Hammerfell

The Redguards, are a human race that primarily inhabits the province of Hammerfell.

Summary:

  • Known for their unique martial culture, they are the most naturally talented warriors in Tamriel.

  • Average human liifespan (70-80 years).

  • Also known for their love of traveling, adventuring, and sailing: they can be seen commonly throughout Tamriel as sailors and mercenaries.

  • Possess a resistance to hot climate and poison, and a fleetness of foot.

  • They are physically blessed with hardy constitutions, able to conquer the hostile environments of Hammerfell.

  • Above average human strength and agility, with tall, gaunt frames and finely toned physiques.


The Redguards are descended from the Yokudans, a seafaring people from the continent of Yokuda, west of Tamriel. When a catastrophic event, often believed to be the sinking of Yokuda, destroyed their homeland, the Yokudans migrated to Tamriel in the First Era. They were fierce warriors and master sailors, bringing their advanced martial traditions with them. After arriving in Hammerfell, they clashed with the native elves, goblins, and human tribes, quickly establishing dominance over the region.

Crowns and Forebears

The arrival of the Yokudans to Hammerfell did not happen all at once; they migrated in waves. The first wave of migrants to arrive on Hammerfell were the Forebears. The Forebears settled the first Redguard cities on Hammerfell, focused on the coastal areas. The latter waves of migrants were known as the Crowns, and they were the old nobility and ruling class of Yokuda. They were the last to leave Yokuda before it sank. The Crowns settled further inland and, being the old rulers of Yokudan society, tend to preserve and cling onto their ancient Yokudan traditions.

For most of their history, the Crown Redguards and the Forebear Redguards were bitter rivals and had many bloody conflicts between them. The Crowns dislike the Forebears because they are more progressive and open to the cultures of other races, forgetting their ancient traditions in favor of tolerating and assimilating to the Empire. The Forebears dislike the Crowns because they view them as just stuck-up conservatives who are stuck in the past.

Religion

Redguards have come to venerate different gods, with the Crowns sticking to the most ancient Yokudan pantheon, while Forebear beliefs have evolved to include many Imperial influences. Many of the Crowns are nomadic tribesman who mainly travel around the Alik'r, and though they adhere to traditional Yokudan beliefs, they have also developed a deep, spiritual attachment to their adopted land.

Crown Gods

  • Satakal: Satakal, also called the World-Snake, is the Yokudan god of everything. He is driven by hunger to eat one world to begin another, a cycle that has happened many times over.

  • Ruptga: Ruptga, more commonly Tall Papa, is the chief deity of the Yokudan and Redguard pantheon. He was the first god to figure out how to survive the Hunger of Satakal.

  • Onsi: A notable warrior god of the Yokudans. Onsi is said to have taught Mankind how to pull their knives into swords.

  • Diagna: The sword-god, he was instrumental to the defeat of the elves native to Hammerfell, as he brought orichalc weapons to the Yokudan people to win the fight.

Forebear Gods

  • Akatosh: Akatosh is the Dragon God of Time who is present in most religions in Tamriel.

  • Julianos: God of Wisdom and Logic, Julianos governs the realms of literature, law, history, and contradiction. He is usually associated with magic, and thus is often revered by wizards.

  • Dibella: Dibella is the Goddess of Beauty, Love, and Affection. She is also acclaimed as the Lady of Love, Beauty, Art, and Music.

  • Stendarr: Stendarr is the God of Mercy, Charity, Well-Earned Luck, and Justice. He is also acclaimed as the God of Compassion, and Righteous Rule by Might and Merciful Forbearance.

Both Pantheons

  • Tava: Tava, The Bird God, is the spirit of the air and goddess of weather in the Redguard pantheon, and has dominion over all elements of sun and storm, save only for the stars, which belong to Ruptga.

  • Tu'whacca: Tu'whacca, the Tricky God, is the Yokudan God of Souls and the Caretaker of the Far Shores, helping Redguards find their way into the afterlife.

  • Morwha: Morwha is the Yokudan goddess of fertility and love. She is a fundamental deity in the Yokudan pantheon and is the favorite wife of Tall Papa.

  • Zeht: Zeht, Yokudan God of Farms, is the Yokudan god of agriculture, civil law, mathematics, and food

The Great War and Ceding from the Empire

During the Great War, Hammerfell became a major battleground between the Aldmeri Dominion and the Empire. The Dominion launched a massive invasion of Hammerfell in 4E 171, overwhelming much of the southern regions, and conquered every city on the souther coast, with the exception of Hegathe.

When the White-Gold Concordat was signed in 4E 175, ending the war between the Empire and the Dominion, one of its terms ceded parts of southern Hammerfell to the Dominion. The Redguards, refusing to accept this concession, rejected the treaty and declared independence from the Empire. This led to the Second War with the Dominion, where the Redguards, united under the Crowns and Forebears, fought a bloody, prolonged conflict to drive the Dominion out of Hammerfell. By 4E 180, the Redguards successfully expelled the Dominion, preserving their homeland and earning a reputation as one of the few provinces to stand against the Thalmor and emerge victorious.
