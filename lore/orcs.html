Orcs / Orsimer


Illustration of an Orc male

The former location of Orsinium

The Orcs, also called Orsimer, are the elves of the Wrothgarian Mountains, Dragontail Mountains, Valenwood, Skyrim, northern Morrowind, and Orsinium.

Summary:

  • Known for their unshakable courage in war and their unflinching endurance of hardships.

  • They have elven blood, but are sometimes grouped into Goblin-ken or considered to be Beastfolk.

  • Lifespan similar to humans, but their war-like nature means they don't usually live long anyway.

  • They have won acceptance in the Empire, in particular for their distinguished service in the Emperor's Legions.

  • Affinity for heavy armor, and can enter into a berserker rage.

  • Orc blacksmiths and craftsmen are prized for their quality.


The closest thing the Orcs have to a "homeland" is probably the city of Orsinium, in the mountains between Skyrim and Hammerfell. But the Orcs are very widespread across Tamriel, and can be found in the cities of other provinces. Most orcs do not live in cities, however, and are instead very tribalistic, living in an Orc Stronghold and belonging to an Orc clan, isolated from the rest of civilization. The Orc tribes in Valenwood or Morrowind will likely have never even heard of the supposed great Orc city of Orsinium

Religion

Malacath, Daedric Prince of Curses

Almost all Orcs follow the worship of one main deity, Malacath, the Daedric Prince of Outcasts, who they revere as their chief god and protector. Malacath embodies resilience, honor, and the hardships of life, reflecting the struggles of the Orcs as a marginalized people in Tamriel. Their religion emphasizes the values of strength, endurance, and loyalty, with a focus on overcoming adversity and maintaining the unity of their stronghold communities. Rituals often involve tests of physical and emotional endurance, as well as strict adherence to the Code of Malacath, which governs their daily lives with principles of justice and honor.
