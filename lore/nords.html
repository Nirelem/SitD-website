Nords


Illustration of a Nord male

Skyrim

The Nords, also called the Children of the Sky are a race of tall, burly and fair-haired humans from Skyrim

Summary:

  • Known for being fierce, strong and enthusiastic warriors.

  • Average human liifespan (70-80 years).

  • They excel in all manner of warfare, and Nord mercenaries are renowned all over Tamriel.

  • Possess an incredible resistance to cold and magical frost.

  • Most Nords seek a glorious death in battle, for those who die valiantly will end up in Sovngarde (AKA Valhalla).

  • Vikings


The Nords' origins are tied to the ancient human migrations from Atmora, a northern, frozen land now mostly lost to time. These early human settlers, known as the Atmorans, arrived in Tamriel around the Merethic Era and the First Era, and eventually spread into what would become Skyrim, developing a hardy, warrior-based society. The Nords were shaped by the harsh climate of Skyrim, their culture emphasizing strength and honor.

The first human settlement in Skyrim was a city called Saarthal. But this city was sacked and razed by the native Snow Elves. In response to this terrible atrocity, Ysgramor and his Five Hundred Companions would sail from Atmora and conquer all of Skyrim from the Snow Elves.

The Nords have a strong warrior culture, with combat skills being highly valued and training starting at a young age. However, they also view magic with distrust and treat any magic-user with suspicion. They live in a clan-based structure, where family ties and loyalty to one’s kin and community are paramount. Cowardice is the worst trait a Nord can exhibit, for "a Nord is judged not by the manner in which he lived, but the manner in which he died".

The Nords are ruled by one High King, but the province is divided into nine holds that are each led by a Jarl. Jarls are the local rulers of holds and their respective capital cities, and are the leaders of the territories the hold encompasses. They govern with a council of Thanes (noble warriors) who offer advice and support to the Jarl's court.

Religion

The Nords are considered to be a devout people with grim religious beliefs. As dark as their belief systems are viewed to be, all Nordic traditions extend one bright, shining hope for Nords: Sovngarde, the Hall of Valor, where Nords who have proven their mettle in battle or died valiantly are welcome to experience euphoric bliss and camaraderie, free from time and boredom.

There are two main pantheons which the Nords follow, both of which consisting of the Aedra (gods): The Imperial Pantheon of the Divines, and the Nordic Pantheon. Most Nords who support the Empire and/or have assimilated into Imperial culture tend to follow the Imperial Pantheon, especially the worship of Talos, the Ninth Divine and God-Hero of Mankind. Those who still follow the Old Ways will likely worship the older Nordic Pantheon.

Nine Divines

  • Akatosh: Akatosh, known as Auri-El to the elves, is the Dragon God of Time and chief deity of the Divines. He represents endurance, stability, and the passage of time and is revered as the father of dragons and a symbol of the Empire’s legitimacy.

  • Arkay: The God of Life and Death, overseeing the natural cycle of birth, life, and the afterlife. He is honored in burial rites and as the protector of the sanctity of life.

  • Dibella: The Goddess of Beauty, Love, and Art. She inspires creativity, passion, and the appreciation of life’s pleasures, often associated with romantic and physical love.

  • Julianos: The God of Wisdom and Logic, revered as a patron of learning, knowledge, and law. He is particularly venerated by scholars, mages, and those who value reason and study.

  • Kynareth: Known as Kyne to the Nords, she is the Goddess of the Winds and Nature. She is the patron of travelers and the natural world, often associated with the sky and the forces of weather.

  • Mara: The Goddess of Love and Compassion, symbolizing familial bonds, marriage, and motherhood. She is widely revered as a nurturing and protective figure.

  • Stendarr: The God of Mercy, Justice, and Righteous Might. He is venerated as a protector of the weak and an upholder of fair judgment and compassion.

  • Zenithar: The God of Work, Commerce, and Prosperity. He represents honest labor and trade, encouraging fairness and success through diligence and integrity.

  • Talos: A mortal and emperor who upon death became a divine, the respect and admiration Imperials had for Tiber Septim is nothing compared to the veneration they have for him as a god of the Nine Divines, regardless of the heresies often espoused about him.

Nordic Pantheon

  • Kyne, the Hawk: Goddess of the storm, widow of Shor, and head of the Nordic pantheon. She gave the men the power of the storm voice.

  • Mara, the She-Wolf: Mother goddess and handmaiden of Kyne.

  • Jhunal, the Owl: God of runes and hermetic orders.

  • Shor, the Fox: Dead god and ruler over Sovngarde.

  • Stuhn, the Whale: Stuhn is the god of ransom, fraternity and justice. He taught the Nords the benefit of taking prisoners of war, and is represented by the whale.

  • Dibella, the Moth: Goddess of beauty and inspiration of artists.

  • Tsun, the Bear: God of trials against adversity. He died defending Shor and became the guardian of the bridge to the Hall of Valor.

  • Orkey, the Snake: The Testing God; not worshiped, rather the Nords guard against them to protect the hearth.

  • Alduin, the Dragon: Dreadful World-Eater, and Twilight God that will devour the world and usher in the next cycle.

The White-Gold Concordat and the Skyrim Civil War

At the end of the Great War, the Empire signed the While-Gold Concordat in order to end the war between the Empire and the Aldmeri Dominion. A major concession included in the treaty was the banning of Talos worship. Talos is widely revered among the Nords of Skyrim, and this caused a large portion of Nords to resent the Empire.

This would eventually lead to the Skyrim Civil War, which took place in Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim, that pitted the western, pro-Empire half of Skyrim against the Stormcloak Rebellion of the eastern half.
